This post proves that the expectation of a positive real-valued random variable $X$ is equal to the integral from $0$ to $\infty$ of one minus cumulative probability of $X$. In symbols,

$$ E(X) = \int_0^\infty (1-F(x)) \ dx. $$

Proof. Denote by $f$ the PDF of $X$, we will start with the right side of the above equation: $$ \begin{align} E(X) &= \int_0^\infty (1-F(x)) \ dx. &= \int_0^\infty \left[ \int_0^\infty f(t)\ dt - \int_0^xf(t)\ dt \right] dx &= \int_0^\infty \int_x^\infty f(t)\ dt\ dx &= \int_0^\infty \int_0^t f(t)\ dx\ dt &= \int_0^\infty f(t)t\ \Big|_0^t\ dt &= \int_0^\infty f(t)t\ dt &=\mathbb{E}(X).\square \end{align} $$
